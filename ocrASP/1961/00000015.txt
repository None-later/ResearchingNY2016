For The Worse

_The recent decision to change the student teaching
grading system from the standard marking procedure to the

ALBANY. NEW YORK. FRIDAY. FEBRUARY 24. 1961

new S-U system (see p. l), to our way of thinking is merely
another example of the educational back-sliding going on
all over the country.

The reasons given for the change seem pretty weak to

us. One reason states that the marking teachers felt they

could not differentiate between A, B, and C students. This

sudden paralysis of the discriminatory facilities seems an
unreasonable precept to ask anyone to accept. Two other
reasons given were: Students should not be working for a

particular grade in student teaching, and, Students would no

longer be under the pressure of grades. To these we answer:
Why shouldn’t a student teacher be working for a specific
grade? Also, if an individual cannot withstand the pressure
of grades, how will he ever withstand the pressure of working

for a salary?

No, we cannot, and will not, go along with this move,
in principle. Of course, we, like everybody else, must subject
ourselves to it — but we shall maintain our position that
this is a grave mistake which does nothing more but relegate
more individual achievement to the huge mass of mediocrity
plaguing our country. A person’s accomplishments deserve
recognition equal to the degree of his success. And if he is
not successful, then he does not deserve even the vicarious
recognition of being lumped into the same category with a
person of a higher calibre. This is exactly what the S-U
system does. It is a poor move. It encourages mediocrity.
