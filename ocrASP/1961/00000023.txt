For the fourth consecutive year, ALBANY has been
stymied in its bid for national recognition. The story runs
much the same as it did last year with the 21 and 6 Sauers-
men succumbing to a powerful Maryland State team in the
District 3I NAIA playoffs. This year the Peds met defeat by
the score of 69-53. At half-time the Marylanders held a 3I-I9
edge.

HOT HAND FROM FIELD

Maryland and Albany played two completely different
brands of ball in the final game held at Jersey City State
Teachers’ College. The Peds played a slow moving, possession
type game much the same as they did against the same team
in losing 55-50 at the NAIA Christmas Tournament. The
Marylanders played their usual running game, but added a
phenomenal shooting percentage of 7I% to their game.
Maryland jumped to a lead of 7-0 in the first several minutes
and were never headed by the ALBANY team. Albany hit
LI'ZGJ from the field but only managed 23 field baskets as
opposed to 28 netted by Maryland. The Peds were out-scored
IIom the charity stripe I3-9.

EARNED FINALS BY ONE POINT
The Sauersmen earned the right to meet Maryland in the
game leading to the national finals at Kansas City by dging
Jersey City State 64-63 on Tuesday night. Don Cohen and
Jim Oppedisano netted 20 each in this winning cause.
Pratt Institute, after losing to Maryland, defeated Jersey
City Stain for third position in the play—offs.
