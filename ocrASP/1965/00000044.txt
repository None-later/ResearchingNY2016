Russian Folk Music
The Don Cossack Chorus seems

to be the most active champions of

Russian folk music.

They still continue to tour the
free world and record their native
music. in a recent trip to Japan,
the old "Sakura," the Japanese
Cherry Blossom Song, was arranged
by conductor Serge Jaroff and is
now featured in the album “On the
River Don."

The “Sakura” sounds strangely
Russian surrounded by a “Siberian
Vagabond Song” and, a familiar
name to readers of “War and
Peace,” Borodin.

made recording history with Maria
Callas' “Carmen,” a stereo “Par-
slfai” and "Melstersinger" with
Jess Thomas, a stereo “Pelleas et
Mellsande," a new “St. Matthew
Passion," “A Magic Flute," but
nothing new by the Beatles. Many
of these albums will be featured
soon in this column.
